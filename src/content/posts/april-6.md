## The Story

The walking trails along the Mirror Lakes and Crow Creek on the Wyoming boarder always result in big zooms and fresh lumber. This is a stick finders dream. 

Today yeiled a long, skinny, but mighty twig. A real knee wrencher none the less.

Big sticks bring big vibes and with the 60 degree weather, the vibes could not have been higher with this find. 

## Stick Fact

Russian Olive (*Elaeagnus angustifolia*), has a surprisingly sneaky superpower: it can fix its own nitrogen from the air. Like legumes, it hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria in nodules on its roots, allowing it to thrive in poor, infertile soils where most trees would struggle — and actually enrich the soil around it in the process.
