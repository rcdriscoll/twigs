## The Story

Targy grabbed it and immediately felt the zoomies take over. Around and around he went, stick in mouth, snow flying, pure joy radiating from every fiber of his being.

## Stick Fact

Cottonwood (*Populus deltoides*) is one of the fastest-growing trees in North America, reaching impressive heights in just a few years. Despite being soft compared to hardwoods like oak or hickory, cottonwood is surprisingly versatile. Native Americans used cottonwood extensively for canoes, shelter, and sacred purposes because of its light weight and workability. It floats easily, resists splitting (thanks to its fibrous grain), and has a natural sweetness that makes it pleasant for extended chewing sessions. It's nature's gift to collectors—abundant, accessible, and full of character.
