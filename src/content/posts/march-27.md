## The Story

Spring has begin to spring in the Black Hills. Good weather usually brings good vibes, with today being no exception. There is a swimming hole that Targy frequents when not occupied by fly fishermen. Not only does the deep run produce 18 inch brown trout eager to munch on some flies. It also producessome of the finest lumber south of I-90. 

The best part about the trove of twigs in the swimming hole is the fetch that ensues.

![Targy wet](/twigs/images/march-27-hero.jpg)

Nothing but straight epicness in todays find. After 20 minutes of cold creek fetch, we had had enough. It is tradition that every peice of lumber out of this hole stays put for other future dogs to find. So our last exit, is almost stickless, but never missing memories.

![Targy vibes](/twigs/images/march-27-zoom.jpg)

## Stick Fact

Cottonwood (*Populus deltoides*), trees release so much "cotton" (their seed fluff) in late spring that it can look like it's snowing and in a bad year it'll pile up on the ground like actual drifts! They're one of the fastest growing trees in North America too, sometimes shooting up 6+ feet in a single year.
